Croissants are a delicious buttery and flaky pastry made from French yeast-leavened laminated dough and is a style of viennoiserie pastries. It was named for its crecent shape, inspired by the Austrian kipferl. The kipferl dates back to the 13th century and comes in various shapes and sizes. The croissant was popularized in France by Marie Antoinette, after requesting the royal bakers to replicate her favorite treat from her Austiran homeland. Then in approximately in 1839, Austrian artillery officer, August Zang, founded a Viennese bakery in Paris. Croissants have been a favorite all across the globe ever since.


Authentic croissants require a methodical approach and lots of patience. Though the ingredients are simple, the process is long and requires lots of attention. Over time though, many have altered the directions and even the ingredients to reduce the time required to make them. They are great as a sweet or savory dish!
